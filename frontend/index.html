Research Output

Publications

First & Corresponding Author
1

Hu, J., Hu, C., Yang, J., Bai, J., & Lee, J. J. (2024). Do traffic flow states follow Markov properties? A high-order spatiotemporal traffic state reconstruction approach for traffic prediction and imputation.

Chaos, Solitons, and Fractals
DOI
Designed a high-order traffic state reconstruction method and proved the Markov property of traffic flow. Leveraged Markov matrix prior knowledge for high-accuracy traffic imputation using an auto-encoder framework.
2

Hu, J., Bai, J., Yang, J., & Lee, J. (2024). Crash Risk Prediction Using Sparse Collision Data: Causal Inference and Graph Convolutional Networks Approaches.

Expert Systems with Applications
DOI
Created a crash data enhancement strategy utilizing historic priori spatial and temporal knowledge. Developed PST-CGCN for predicting crash risk based on causal inference and graph convolutional networks.
3

Hu, J., Zhang, J., Bai, J., & Lee, J. (2025). Dynamic Correlation Analysis of Urban Crashes Using Tucker-Net Based SIRS Model: A Case Study in New York City.

Journal of the Franklin Institute
DOI
Designed TNBSM — a data-driven and transferable analysis model using tensor decomposition and the SIRS model to explore dynamic correlations within crash zones.
4

Hu, J., & Lee, J. (2025). Car following dynamics in mixed traffic flow of autonomous and human-driven vehicles: complex networks approach.

Physica A: Statistical Mechanics and its Applications
DOI
Applied a coarse-grained phase space algorithm, introduced complex network technology into vehicle-following behavior analysis, and revealed distinct characteristics between AV and HV.
5

Hu, J., Gao, D., Lee, J., & Wang, L. (2025). Vehicle dynamics analytics based on complex network techniques: a trajectory-based visibility graph approach.

Chaos, Solitons, and Fractals
DOI
Introduced an adaptive trajectory-based visibility graph (TVG) framework, transforming planar trajectory data into complex networks with a tunable visibility tolerance coefficient.
6

Hu, J., Bai, J., & Lee, J. (2026). Simplified and Efficient KNN-Based Method for High-Resolution Traffic Time-Space Diagram Imputation.

Transportation Research Record
Proposed a modified KNN framework for reconstructing Time-Space Diagrams from sparse data by matching spatiotemporal neighborhood features defined by Queen Contiguity Spatial Rule.
7

Hu, J., Gao, D., Hu, C., Zhou, H., & Lee, J. (2026). Rethinking driving style recognition: A prediction error-based driving behavior modeling.

Advanced Engineering Informatics
Revisited end-to-end driving style recognition by exploring driver behavior heterogeneity through trajectory prediction errors. Introduced a multi-modal distribution for future trajectories based on driving style.
8

Hu, J., Lee, J., & Wang, L. (2026). Re-examining the Explanatory Boundaries of Car-Following Models: From a Systematic Decomposition of Fitting Errors to the Revelation of Adaptive Feedback Mechanisms.

Reliability Engineering & System Safety
Posited that CF model residuals are a composite of structural and random errors; utilized an Unobserved Components Model to decompose residual series from three calibrated CF models.
9

Hu, J., Bai, J., & Lee, J. (2026). Meta-Learning Guided by Physics-Based Residual Priors: A Plug-and-Play Training Strategy for Lightweight Car following Models.

Transportation Research Part C: Emerging Technologies
Introduced RAMR — a plug-and-play framework that reframes CF model training as a bilevel optimization task, validated across four datasets with three physical CF models and three data-driven predictors.
10

Wang, L., Lee, J., Hu, J*. (2026). The Resilience Benefit of Disorder: A Global Typological Analysis of Road Network Morphology and Urban Performance.

Sustainable Cities and Society
Using a global dataset of 70 cities, investigated how road network morphology (directional entropy) affects urban sustainability metrics including CO2 emissions, commute times, and congestion.
